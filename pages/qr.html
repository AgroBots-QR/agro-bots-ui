How QR Codes Work

A QR code (Quick Response Code) is a two-dimensional barcode that can store data, such as text or a URL, and be easily scanned by a smartphone or QR code reader. Here's how it works:

  1. Encoding: Information is encoded into a QR code using a combination of black squares arranged on a white background.
  2. Scanning: To read a QR code, a smartphone or QR code reader captures an image of the code.
  3. Decoding: The scanning device decodes the pattern of black squares to retrieve the encoded information.
  4. Action: Depending on the content of the QR code, the scanning device may perform various actions, such as opening a website, displaying text, or initiating a payment.

QR codes are widely used for various purposes, including:

  • Product packaging
  • Marketing campaigns
  • Boarding passes and tickets
  • Mobile payments
  • Authentication and security

They offer a convenient way to share information digitally and bridge the physical and digital worlds.
